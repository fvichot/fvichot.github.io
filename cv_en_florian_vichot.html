Articles

IpMorph: fingerprinting spoofing unificationJournal in computer virology 6, no. 4 (2010)2010
Authors: G. Prigent, F. Vichot, F. Harrouet
Nowadays, there is a variety of tools for easily identifying the TCP/IP stack's fingerprint of a target machine. IpMorph allows concealing this fingerprint, and even mimicking the fingerprint of a chosen TCP/IP stack. This is done through session tracking and packet rewriting on the fly. We also detail its effectiveness against tools such as Nmap, Xprobe2, Ring2, SinFP and p0f.
Cardiac Interventional Guidance using Multimodal Data Processing and Visualisation: medInria as an Interoperability PlatformMidas Journal2012
Authors: F. Vichot, H. Cochet, B. Bleuzé, N. Toussaint, P. Jaïs, M. Sermesant
MedInria is a medical images software developed at Inria, which aims to provide clinicians with state-of-the-art algorithms for processing and visualising their images. In this article, we will focus on its use in pre-surgery preparation for cardiac interventions, and the difficulties arising from the lack of standardisation of certain data formats and visualisation conventions.
